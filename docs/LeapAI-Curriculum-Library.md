# LeapAI — Prebuilt Curriculum Library
### 20 Lessons | 4 Pillars | 5 Age Bands | Ready to Launch

---

> **How to use:** Each lesson below includes the one-click OpenMAIC prompt.
> Paste the prompt into the LeapAI classroom generator and click Generate.
> A full interactive lesson with slides, quiz, and simulation will be ready in ~60 seconds.

---

## 🟡 PILLAR 1: LEARN AI
*"What is AI and how does it work?"*

### Ages 5–8 — "Meet the Robots!"
**Prompt:** *"Teach a 6-year-old what artificial intelligence is, using friendly animals and toys as examples. Include a short quiz and a fun drawing activity. Keep all language simple and encouraging."*

### Ages 9–13 — "How Does AI Think?"
**Prompt:** *"Create an interactive lesson for a 12-year-old explaining how AI learns from data, using a dog-recognition example. Include a simulation showing how more training data improves accuracy, and a quiz."*

### Ages 14–18 — "AI, Machine Learning & Neural Networks"
**Prompt:** *"Teach a 16-year-old the difference between AI, machine learning, and neural networks. Include a roundtable debate: 'Is AI really intelligent?' and a project activity where students design a simple AI system."*

### Ages 19–64 — "AI at Work: What You Need to Know"
**Prompt:** *"Create a 30-minute workplace AI literacy lesson for adults. Explain how AI is changing jobs, what automation means for workers, and how to use AI tools safely at work. Include a workflow redesign activity."*

### Ages 65+ — "Understanding AI in Everyday Life"
**Prompt:** *"Teach a senior citizen what AI is using examples from daily life — voice assistants, Netflix recommendations, GPS navigation. Keep language simple and reassuring. Include a quiz with real-world examples."*

## 🟢 PILLAR 2: USE AI
*"How do I use AI tools well?"*

### Ages 5–8 — "Ask the AI Helper"
**Prompt:** *"Teach a 7-year-old how to ask an AI assistant good questions. Use simple examples like asking for a bedtime story or help with a school drawing. Include a 'good question vs. bad question' game."*

### Ages 9–13 — "AI for School: Tools, Tips & Rules"
**Prompt:** *"Create a lesson for middle schoolers on using AI for homework, writing, and research — including rules about honesty, citation, and when NOT to use AI. Include a 'responsible AI use' quiz."*

### Ages 14–18 — "AI as Your Creative Partner"
**Prompt:** *"Teach high school students how to use AI for writing, coding, art, and music — including how to evaluate AI outputs critically, cite AI assistance honestly, and maintain their own creative voice."*

### Ages 19–64 — "Using AI to Run Your Small Business"
**Prompt:** *"Create a hands-on lesson for small business owners on using AI tools for marketing, customer service, scheduling, and finance. Include a PBL activity: build an AI-powered customer FAQ bot."*

### Ages 65+ — "Using Voice Assistants & AI Apps Safely"
**Prompt:** *"Teach seniors how to use voice assistants (Alexa, Siri, Google Assistant) and health AI apps safely and confidently. Include a step-by-step simulation and a list of what to share vs. never share with AI."*

## 🔵 PILLAR 3: CREATE AI
*"How is AI built?"*

### Ages 5–8 — "Train Your AI Pet!"
**Prompt:** *"Teach a 6-year-old how AI learns using a simple 'train your pet robot' game analogy. Include a fun interactive activity where the student 'teaches' an AI by showing it examples, and sees it improve."*

### Ages 9–13 — "Build a Simple AI Classifier"
**Prompt:** *"Create a hands-on lesson for a 12-year-old on how to build a simple image classifier using visual block-based coding concepts. Include a simulation where students train and test the classifier."*

### Ages 14–18 — "Intro to Machine Learning & Python AI"
**Prompt:** *"Teach a high schooler how to build a basic ML model in Python. Cover data collection, training, testing, and evaluation. Include a project: 'Build an AI that recognizes handwritten digits.'"*

### Ages 19–64 — "No-Code AI: Build Your First AI Tool"
**Prompt:** *"Teach an adult with no coding background how to build a no-code AI app — either a chatbot or an image classifier — using free tools. Include a PBL activity with a working prototype by end of lesson."*

### Ages 65+ — "How People Build AI: Behind the Scenes"
**Prompt:** *"Explain to a senior how AI systems are designed and built, without any coding. Focus on the human decisions involved — what data is used, who sets the rules, and how to hold AI accountable."*

## 🔴 PILLAR 4: PROTECT
*"How do I stay safe and protect my rights?"*

### Ages 5–8 — "Real or Pretend? Spotting Fake Stuff Online"
**Prompt:** *"Teach a 7-year-old how to tell if a photo or video is real or fake. Use simple, age-appropriate examples. Include a quiz with 'real or fake?' picture questions and a rule they can tell their parents."*

### Ages 9–13 — "Deepfakes, Data & Digital Rights"
**Prompt:** *"Create a lesson for a 12-year-old on deepfakes, data privacy, and their digital rights. Include a simulation: 'Spot the deepfake' and a discussion: 'What can you do if AI is used to hurt someone?'"*

### Ages 14–18 — "AI Ethics, Bias & Your Civil Rights"
**Prompt:** *"Teach a 16-year-old about AI bias, algorithmic discrimination, and how to advocate for fair AI systems. Include a roundtable debate and a real-world case study on biased AI in hiring or policing."*

### Ages 19–64 — "AI Scams, Fraud & How to Fight Back"
**Prompt:** *"Create a workplace-focused lesson for adults on AI-enabled impersonation scams, voice cloning fraud, and AI phishing. Include real case examples, a quiz, and a 5-step response plan."*

### Ages 65+ — "Protecting Yourself from AI Scams"
**Prompt:** *"Teach seniors how to recognize and avoid AI-powered scams — robocalls, voice-clone family emergency scams, fake Medicare calls, and AI phishing emails. Include a simulation and a printed action card to keep by the phone."*
