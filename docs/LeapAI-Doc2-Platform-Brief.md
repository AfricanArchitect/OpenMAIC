### Document 2 of 5 | March 2026

## Executive Summary

LeapAI is Super Intelligence Agency and the world's first national lifelong AI learning and workforce transition infrastructure, built on three
interdependent pillars: an open-source AI classroom platform (powered by OpenMAIC), a portable
digital credential system (the LeapAI Learning Passport), and a portable funding mechanism
(Lifelong Learning Accounts). Together, these pillars address the structural root causes of the
AI readiness gap — not as a training course, but as a re-architecting of how education, work,
and income security are connected.

---

## Part 1: The Crisis — Naming the Problem

### The Readiness Gap / AI Adoption-Readiness Lag
Dominates business and policy discussions. Describes the disconnect between organizational
investment in AI infrastructure and the workforce's lack of foundational skills to use it.
90% of organizations believe they have the tools to scale innovation, but only 29% of leaders
feel their workforce is ready to leverage AI effectively (Kyndryl 2025 Readiness Report).

### The Jobpocalypse
A media-driven portmanteau gaining traction since late 2025. Reflects public anxiety over
structural unemployment, particularly for entry-level white-collar workers. Anthropic CEO
Dario Amodei warned that AI could eliminate half of entry-level white-collar jobs in the US
within five years. The language matters: "readiness gap" is used in boardrooms;
"Jobpocalypse" captures the psychological impact on workers.

## Part 2: Core Bottlenecks LeapAI Addresses

### 1. Insufficient and Misaligned Reskilling Programs
84% of employees have not received a single AI learning opportunity in the past year (Dayforce 2025),
even though 63% want to develop AI skills. While 82% of executives agree companies should reskill
impacted workers, only 17% of organizations actually do so.

**LeapAI Response:** Prebuilt curriculum library with 20 lessons across 4 pillars and 5 age bands.
Emphasis on "Process Pro" skills — identifying friction points and deploying AI to solve them.

### 2. Incentive Misalignments
Employers prefer to "buy" talent rather than "build" it. Governments are slow to update curricula.
Educational institutions push four-year degrees when the market demands short-cycle, stackable credentials.

**LeapAI Response:** Lifelong Learning Accounts make the funding continuous and owned by the worker.
Tax incentives for "earn-and-learn" employers. State-adaptable deployment with no federal mandate.

### 3. The Speed Asymmetry
AI capability advances on an exponential curve; human retraining follows a linear path.

**LeapAI Response:** Dynamic Curriculum Layer with quarterly updates. Stackable micro-credentials
updatable in weeks. Work-integrated learning as the default model, not an alternative track.

### 4. Vulnerability Across Age Groups
- **Older workers (50+):** Age discrimination + lack of digital literacy baseline
- **Younger workers (under 25):** Entry-level roles being automated first; lose the learning ladder

**LeapAI Response:** Dedicated age-band tracks for every cohort with age-appropriate UX and safety controls.

### 2026–2030: The Great Deconstruction
- Process Pro track: workflow redesign using AI

## Part 4: The Three-Pillar Solution

### Pillar A: LeapAI Platform
**Tech:** OpenMAIC — Next.js 16, React 19, TypeScript, LangGraph 1.1, Tailwind CSS 4
**License:** AGPL-3.0
**LLMs:** OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini, DeepSeek, Grok, any OpenAI-compatible API
**Deploy:** Vercel (one-click) or Docker (self-hosted)

### Pillar B: LeapAI Learning Passport
- Portable digital credential wallet
- Stackable micro-credentials earned in weeks
- Industry-verified, not legacy-accreditation-gated
- Federal aid eligible
- U.S. answer to China's Credit Bank (Guangdong, 2026)

### Pillar C: Lifelong Learning Accounts
- $5,000–$10,000 seed, ages 16–99
- Replenished: employer + individual (tax-incentivized) + government
- Worker-owned and portable between jobs
- Not a federal mandate — state pilot model first
